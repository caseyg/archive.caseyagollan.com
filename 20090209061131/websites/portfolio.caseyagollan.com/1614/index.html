I love being part of Zephyr because it combines my passion for art with my love for design. Though it is only published once a year, when printer deadlines start approaching, it becomes a full time job. In the final production week, it's not uncommon to find us working from 3pm to 10pm, when the custodians kick us out of school.

At a school where there is an endless stream of pep rallies honoring our varsity athletes, I am proud to promote the incredible, yet underrepresented artists and authors. I have been a part of Zephyr since I was a freshman, when I took charge with a total redesign of the magazine and update of the old software. I am currently serving my second year as Editor-in-Chief. I oversee and am involved in selecting writing and artwork, designing the magazine, promoting the arts, and distributing the finished product.
