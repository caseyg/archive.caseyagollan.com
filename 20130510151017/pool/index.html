Pool

Website, Coding, Publications

Visit Project

Eight months after contributing a piece of writing to Pool, a publication about internet art, and one passive-agressive message later I ended up working with Founder and Lead Editor Louis Doulas to develop the site's second iteration. While staying true to Louis's intensely stark mockups, we arrived at a better reading experience and managed to make a ton of subtle, under-the-hood improvements to the site's functionality. The site's homepage bares a list of everything ever published by Pool, with visited links shown in gray. This allows readers to leave and return to the site and remember what is left to be explored.

Some of the improvements we made behinds the scenes:

  • Designed the site responsively so that it scales gracefully and reads great on everything from iPhones to iPads to gigantic desktops.
  • Cleaned out thousands of spam comments and took measures to make sure they don't come back.
  • Turned on caching to speed up site performance.
  • Humanized the site's permalinks so they're shorter and easier to read.
  • Created a more visual PDF page that puts Rasmus Svensson's smart designs on display.
  • Reformatted lots of old content to display captions and author information in more semantic and structured ways.
